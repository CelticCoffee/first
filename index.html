Goals

In the fall, I enrolled in the Edx online course through Harvard called CS50 (introduction to computer science). Having spent my professional life to date as a violinist, composer and small business owner, I did not expect to either like or enjoy the course but much to my amazement, I loved it. My initial interests were in pivoting careers into the field of graphic design in order to retain a heavy artistic component to my professional life. But after the experience of beginning to code in C, I realized that I have a great fascination with what is going on under the hood.

I don't just want to make it look pretty, I want to make it do things. As a small business owner, I have both benefited from and banged my head against walls for lack of savvy technology to meet my needs. Countless times I have wished that I had the skills to create something the way I wanted it to be on the web. These days it is critically important in business, the arts and most every other genre to be able to represent oneself impressively online.

I am here in this class because I am determined to become a developer and while there are lots of good online resources, I want to begin to connect with people in the industry, to see what the collaborative side of life in the developer world is like and to have the accountability of a formal program to help me move towards my goal. I have no illusion that I'll leave this first class with all of the tools necessary but I am hoping to be on that journey by beginning a portfolio of work that will open doors for future educational opportunities be they internships, further schooling or strong professional connections.
